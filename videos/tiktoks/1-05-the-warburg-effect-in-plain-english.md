**Full voiceover:**
"The Warburg effect is often summarized as cancer loves sugar. True, but incomplete. A growing tumor is not just trying to make energy. It is trying to build new cells. Glycolysis can feed pathways that help make DNA, membranes, proteins, and signaling molecules. Low oxygen and growth signaling push this rewiring further. And glucose is not the only input. Tumors may depend on glutamine, lipids, or other amino acids. Cancer metabolism asks: what does the tumor need, where does it get it, and what bottleneck can therapy exploit?"
