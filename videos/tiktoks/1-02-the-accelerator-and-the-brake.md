## TikTok 1.2 — The Accelerator and the Brake

**Book:** Cancer Biology  
**Chapters:** 4-6  
**Length target:** 55 seconds  
**Learning promise:** viewers can distinguish oncogenes from tumor suppressor genes.

**Hook:** "Cancer often needs an accelerator stuck down and a brake cut."

**Shot-by-shot timeline:**

| Time | Visual | Voiceover | On-screen text |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0-3 | Toy car or simple car animation races forward. | "Cancer genetics has a simple first metaphor." | Accelerator + brake |
| 3-11 | Foot presses accelerator; label "oncogene." | "Oncogenes are like accelerators. A normal growth gene gets stuck in the on position." | Oncogene = stuck accelerator |
| 11-21 | Brake line snaps; label "tumor suppressor." | "Tumor suppressor genes are brakes. They slow division, repair damage, or trigger death." | Tumor suppressor = broken brake |
| 21-33 | Split screen: RAS/MYC on one side, TP53/RB1/BRCA on the other. | "RAS and MYC are classic accelerator stories. TP53, RB1, BRCA1, and BRCA2 are classic brake and repair stories." | RAS / MYC / TP53 / RB1 / BRCA |
| 33-45 | Stack of mutation cards accumulates. | "Most cancers are not one mutation. They are a stack of advantages selected over time." | Cancer accumulates advantages |
| 45-55 | Cell clone expands; normal cell fades behind it. | "That stack lets a cell grow, survive, and copy damage when it should stop." | Growth + survival + instability |

**Full voiceover:**
"Cancer genetics has a simple first metaphor: an accelerator stuck down and a brake cut. Oncogenes are accelerators. A normal proto-oncogene helps a cell grow or survive, but when it is mutated or overactive, it can push growth too hard. Tumor suppressor genes are brakes. They slow division, repair DNA damage, or trigger death when a cell is unsafe. RAS and MYC are classic accelerator stories. TP53, RB1, BRCA1, and BRCA2 are classic brake and repair stories. Most cancers are not one mutation. They are a stack of advantages selected over time."

**Caption copy:** Oncogenes push. Tumor suppressors restrain. Cancer often needs both kinds of failure.
