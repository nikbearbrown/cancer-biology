## TikTok 1.1 — Cancer Is Not One Disease

**Book:** Cancer Biology  
**Chapters:** 1-2  
**Length target:** 60 seconds  
**Audience:** first-time cancer biology learner  
**Learning promise:** by the end, viewers understand cancer as deregulated cell behavior, not a single disease.

**Hook:** "Cancer is not one disease. It is one kind of failure."

**Shot-by-shot timeline:**

**Full voiceover:**
"Cancer is not one disease. It is what happens when a cell stops obeying the rules that make a body possible. A normal cell divides when signaled, repairs damage, dies when damage is too dangerous, stays in its tissue, and cooperates with neighbors. Cancer begins when those controls fail. Not all at once, not in the same order, but enough that the cell starts behaving for itself. That is why leukemia, melanoma, lung cancer, and glioblastoma are not interchangeable. They break different systems in different tissues. But the shared logic is this: normal regulation fails, and evolution starts operating inside the body."

**Caption copy:** Cancer is best understood as deregulated cellular behavior. The vocabulary gets much easier after that.
