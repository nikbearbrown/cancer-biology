**Full voiceover:**
"A damaged cell is supposed to have an exit plan. Apoptosis is programmed cell death: controlled demolition, not chaos. If DNA damage is too severe, internal stress pathways can trigger death. External death signals can also tell unsafe cells to shut down. Cancer cells evade this by disabling p53, increasing survival proteins, or blocking death signals. That is why some therapies work by restoring the death program rather than simply poisoning the cell. In cancer biology, growth is only half the story. Survival is the other half."
